About

Born and raised in Japan, I developed an interest in creative activities and explored various experiences, including studying ceramics. After moving to Germany I dedicated three years to focused study in the field.

The approach to ceramics in Japan and Germany differs significantly, and as Germany is an industrialized country, I had the valuable opportunity to learn in detail about everything from materials to distribution.

Although I have lived and created in Germany for a long time, my sense of aesthetics and philosophy are fundamentally rooted in Japan.

Philosophy

Ceramics can be categorized either as art or craft, but I do not believe it necessarily fits into either category exclusively.

Around 100 years ago, a movement in Japan, known as Mingei (The Arts and Crafts Movement), began to seek beauty in everyday life.

I believe that the beauty basics needed for daily life are what allow for the continuation of a rich existence, and it is this philosophy that drives my creative work.
