About

[Artist Portrait]

I was born and raised in Japan, where I developed an interest in creative activities and explored various experiences, including studying ceramics. After moving to Germany I dedicated three years to focused study in the field.

The approach to ceramics in Japan and Germany differs significantly, and as Germany is an industrialized country, I had the valuable opportunity to learn in detail about everything from materials to distribution. Although I have lived and created in Germany for a long time, my sense of aesthetics and philosophy are fundamentally rooted in Japan.

Ceramics can be categorized either as art or craft, but I do not believe it necessarily fits into either category exclusively. Around 100 years ago, a movement in Japan, known as Mingei (The Arts and Crafts Movement), began to seek beauty in everyday life. I believe that the beauty basics needed for daily life are what allow for the continuation of a rich existence, and it is this philosophy that drives my creative work.

Skills & Techniques

Wheel Throwing

Proficient in wheel throwing techniques, able to create a wide range of functional and artistic ceramics with precision and attention to detail.

Glaze Making

I have created and combined several original glazes, and since the possibilities for glazes are endless, I will continue to experiment with new combinations in the future.

Precision & Focus

Able to produce over 100 identical pieces in an 8-hour workday with a margin of error of just 2mm, demonstrating exceptional concentration and precision.
